Hi this is Regina! I am a researcher in HCI, computational social science and learning technology. I am currently a 3rd year PhD student in the Human Centered Design & Engineering (HCDE) department at University of Washington, advised by Dr. Benjamin Mako Hill and Dr. Jennifer Turns. I am proud to be part of the Community Data Science Collective research group.

My research is centered on understanding and designing to support informal learning in online creative communities. I use mixed-method approaches to study learners' motivation, challenges and opportunities around learning activities such as feedback-seeking and knowledge building in a variety of online communities, including fanfiction groups, design critique forum and data science competition platform.

Currently, I am focusing on data science communities for novices. I am working with Dr. Benjamin Mako Hill and Dr. Sayamindu Dasgupta on a new line of research where we seek to understand novices' engagement with data and design to scaffold them in creative and social activities that can help them develop data literacy.
